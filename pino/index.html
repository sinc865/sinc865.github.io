Abstract

Generating realistic group interactions involving multiple characters remains challenging as group size expands, because interaction complexity grows combinatorially. Existing conditional diffusion models tackle this by incrementally generating motions conditioned on previously generated characters, but they rely on a single shared prompt; this limits nuanced control and often yields oversimplified interactions.

We introduce Person-Interaction Noise Optimization (PINO), a novel, training-free framework for generating realistic and customizable interactions among groups of arbitrary size. PINO decomposes complex group behavior into a sequence of semantically relevant pairwise interactions and leverages pretrained two-person interaction diffusion models. During noise optimization, physics-based penalties ensure physical plausibility by preventing artifacts such as character overlap or penetration, while affording precise user control over orientation, speed, and spatial relationships—without any additional training.

Comprehensive evaluations show that PINO produces visually realistic, physically coherent, and highly adaptable multi-person interactions, making it well suited for diverse applications in animation, gaming, and robotics.

Approach

PINO addresses multi-character interaction by decomposing it into a series of pairwise motion generation steps.

  1. It starts from a pretrained two-person diffusion model, without requiring further training.
  2. To add a new character, the method forms a pair with an existing one, provides a text prompt describing only that pair, and generates their motion using the model.
  3. The initial noise is then optimized to ensure that the generated motion is consistent with the full group—avoiding overlap, maintaining appropriate distances, orientations.
  4. This process is repeated iteratively to construct interactions involving arbitrary numbers of characters. A similar procedure can also be applied to extend motion sequences.

Because physical and spatial penalties are enforced during the noise optimization step, PINO allows fine control over motion composition while keeping the diffusion model unchanged.
